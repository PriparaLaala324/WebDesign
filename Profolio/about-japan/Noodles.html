NOODLES

Do you know that noodles isn't originally come from Japan?

Japanese ramen originated from Cantonese noodle soup in Guangdong, China.

It first appeared in the Edo period in Japan.

It was introduced by Chinese Cantonese chefs brought by Zhu Shunshui, a relic of the Ming Dynasty, in Nagasaki, Japan.

However, it is only spread among a few nobles in Japan.

The main popular pasta in Japan is still local soba noodles, and ramen has not sprouted in Japan.

It was not until the Meiji period that Chinese Cantonese chefs came to Japanese ports such as Yokohama, Kobe, and Nagasaki to introduce their hometown pasta for the second time, and ramen began to sprout in Japan.
