TAKOYAKI

The history of takoyaki dates back to the Taisho era. It has the characteristics of crispy skin and tender meat, it's delicious and cheap. It is a well-known national quintessence snack in Japan.

Takoyaki, also known as octopus balls, has a history of more than 80 years in Japan.

It is a kind of snack that has been popular in Japan for a long time. It is said that takoyaki first originated from the founder of Aizuya, a restaurant specializing in takoyaki in Osaka.

At first, Ryuki Endo added meat, konjac, etc. to the prepared wheat flour batter and fry it, and then sold it at food stalls.

Later, in 1935, Ryuki Endo started to use octopus as a raw material and added condiments to the batter, and the fried takoyaki balls became very popular.
