SUKIYAKI

A Japanese dish that is a hot pot where ingredients are cooked in a small amount of sauce. Usually the ingredients include beef slices, green onions, lettuce, tofu, konjac, mushrooms, etc. These ingredients are boiled in a soup mixed with soy sauce, sugar and mirin, and eaten with raw eggs.

In 1859, Yokohama, an overseas trading port, introduced the food culture of eating beef abroad, and the izakaya "Ise Kuma" in Yokohama began to serve beef dishes. Although the dishes at that time were called "beef pot", it is said to be Sukiyaki. The origin of burning.

Later, after the Great Kanto Earthquake in 1923, the use of WARISITA sauce in barbecues began to spread around the world.
